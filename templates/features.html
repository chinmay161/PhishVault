PhishVault Features

Risk Score

The Risk Score ranges from 0 to 100, summarizing how dangerous a URL is:
  • 0–30: Likely Safe
  • 31–70: Suspicious
  • 71–100: High Risk / Confirmed Phishing
Calculated from the combination of threat database checks, detection indicators, and technical signals.

Detection Indicators

These are patterns in the URL or domain that are commonly associated with phishing attempts:

IndicatorExplanation
IP Address in URLInstead of a domain, raw IPs are used, often malicious.
URL EncodingEncoded characters (%2F, %3D) used to obfuscate the destination.
Too Many SubdomainsTries to mimic legit domains (e.g. secure-login.paypal.com.fake.com).
Mismatch DomainBrand name in domain doesn't match actual host.

Threat Database Checks

These checks compare the URL against well-known phishing and malware databases:

DatabaseDescription
Google Safe BrowsingGoogle's blacklist of known phishing and malware sites.
PhishTankCommunity-sourced and verified phishing URLs.

Technical Analysis

Inspects the web page content to detect suspicious behavior or scripts:

FeatureRisk Explanation
iFramesCan be used to silently load phishing content from other domains.
Obfuscated JavaScriptCommon in phishing pages to hide malicious actions.
External Form ActionForms submitting credentials to unknown domains.
Fake SSL IconsMisleads users into thinking the page is secure.
